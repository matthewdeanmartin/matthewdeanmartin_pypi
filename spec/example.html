My Open Source Philosophy

When you use one of my packages, you aren't just downloading code; you're entering into a relationship of trust. I believe that trust is earned. Here is my commitment to you.

1. Quality is Verifiable

Every stable release you use has passed a rigorous set of checks. I believe in high test coverage, and I bundle the coverage reports in my packages so you can verify it for yourself.

2. Documentation is a Feature

If it's not documented, it doesn't exist. I strive to provide clear, example-driven documentation for all public APIs. If you find something confusing, that's a bug in the docs, and I encourage you to file an issue.

3. Clear Licensing and Governance

You will never have to guess about your legal rights to use my code. All my projects use standard, permissive OSI-approved licenses (primarily MIT or Apache 2.0). You can find the LICENSE file in the root of this package.

4. Responsive & Respectful Maintenance

I am actively involved in the maintenance of my key projects. The low "open issue" count on my projects isn't an accident; it's a policy. I promise to review and respond to every bug report in a timely and respectful manner.

5. Long-Term Stability & Security

I follow Semantic Versioning strictly. I will not introduce breaking changes without a major version bump. Security patches (like the one in AuthKit v1.4.2) are my highest priority.
